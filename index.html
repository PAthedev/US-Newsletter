Opinion / Taylor Williamson

Reviving the City

What is a viable strategy to try and bring life back to your city in the midst of a worldwide pandemic? Chicago has begun releasing video ads that feature sweeping views of empty tourist attractions and parts of the city that were formally frequented daily by thousands of Chicago natives and tourists alike. The ads are produced by Chicago's tourist center. They are a reminder that the country's major cities rely on the electric atmosphere created by the residents and visitors who fill these public spaces.

For the past year, stay at home orders have presented Chicago's city marketing departments with the challenge of reviving the city's tourism and commercial economies. This can also be looked at as an opportunity. It's a chance to restart and rebrand the city and it's attractions. Though they, haven't been made public, Chicago's tourism department has created plan for tourism revival and the next steps to bring the city back to life. While tourism holds a significant share of the economy in Chicago, The city has also not lost sight of the fact that there must be a great deal of effort afforded to retaining quality residents. This is especially important in light studies which are suggesting that people are looking to move to less dense areas following the pandemic.

Politics / Los Angeles

Pursuit of happiness

The Los Angeles Health Report Agency has released a report that addresses the question that many governments are strugging to answer themselves: how do you ensure less Covid infections and balance that out with the social price of being locked down for an extended period of time. The goal of the report is to measure the effects of various by-products of lockdown including isolation from friends and family. "Loneliness seems to be one of the biggest consequences of the lockdown," says James Petes, a senior researcher for the Los Angeles Health Report Agency. "We've also found that that extended periods of loneliness and isolation has a profound effect on those who are already suffering from anxiexy." Because of this, the report suggest that it could be beneficial for the well being of your community to safely check in with your neighbors to ensure their well-being.

Business / USA

Troubled waters

Florida based Carnival Cruise Line has made an announcement that it plans to return to business within the next 2 months. Initially, 8 ships will be launched from Texas and Florida. The question is, are consumers ready to return to an enclosed area like a ship this soon after re-opening and still in the midst of a nationwide pandemic?

Culture / New York

Bedtime reading

Manhattan based bookshop The Corridor has announced that its Broadway Ave. Upper West Side location will become a 24 hour location. The shop is a popular bookstore and walking distance from Central Park. Regulars of the popular bookstore have frequently inquired as to when the bookstore was re-opening and whether or not it would once again return to being a 24 hour location after it's hours were heavily reduced in the midst of the pandemic. The Corridor plans to add a new foreign-language section to help cater to it's frequent tourist guest who come from countries all over the world. In addition to this, The Corridor's coffee shop has been expanded and doubled in size to accomodate the hundreds of guests who frequent the bookstore every day.

Society / San Francisco

Nightclub heroes

San Francisco's nightlife has long faced an existential crisis from rapid gentrification, which is transforming clubs into condominiums. So when venues were shuttered in mid-March – putting some 9,000 people out of work – it came as a particularly devastating blow to the already beleaguered sector. Springing to the rescue was Clubcommission, the entity representing Berlin’s clubs, which launched the online platform United We Stream as a way of bringing live DJ sets from some of the city’s most iconic venues into the homes of those in lockdown. “We knew we’d have to act quickly,” says spokesperson Fred Kantz. “We now have about 60 clubs involved.” Viewers can donate by buying merchandise and virtual beers; they’ve already raised some €430,000 for a relief fund. But Kantz says that the initiative, which has spread to other American cities, is also about starting a wider conversation. “We have [also] launched United We Talk, which livestreams roundtables on club culture and its place in society.” For nightlife-crazy San Francisco, that’s a conversation worth having.
